Program Overview
⌄

The DOW (Department of Defense) program extends Varsity Tutors live chat tutoring to military-connected families across three regions. This model forecasts how many tutors need to be contracted — by region — to handle projected demand at the November 1, 2026 launch date.

RegionStudent populationShareTutor sourceSubject scope

The 29 subjects span six groups: Elementary (5), Middle School (5), HS Math (5), HS Humanities (2), HS Science (4), Test Prep (2), Special Ed (1), and Languages (5 — with region-specific availability).

Spanish is available in the US and Europe only — excluded from Asia as it is not relevant for military families based in Japan or South Korea.
2
How Demand Is Calculated
⌄

For every subject and every region, the model calculates how many live chat sessions per week will be needed at launch. Demand is split into two independent levers: penetration rate (what % of students ever use live chat) and sessions per active user (how often they use it).

Core demand formula Weekly sessions = Student population × Penetration rate × Sessions per active user/yr × Subject weight ÷ 52

Separating penetration from frequency lets you stress-test each assumption independently. "5% of students using it 20 times" and "20% of students using it 5 times" produce the same total demand but very different tutor profile needs.

Subject demand weights

Each subject's demand share is derived from a blended pool of 84,686 Instant Chat (IC) sessions and 16,852 IV requests — a combined pool of 91,658 data points across 29 DOW subjects. IC data is used as the primary source for the 20 subjects where it's available, since IC sessions are the most direct proxy for live chat demand. For the 9 subjects with no IC history (Pre-Algebra, SAT, ACT, Exec. Func., and all 5 language subjects), IV counts are scaled to IC equivalents using the ratio between the two datasets.

Why this matters: The IC data significantly shifted weights vs IV alone. Middle School Math rose from 9.8% to 23.0% of demand — it dominates actual chat sessions far more than IV suggested. Algebra 2, Pre-Calculus, HS Chemistry and HS Physics all dropped substantially, as students with those problems tend to prefer instant video over chat. HS English, MS Writing and HS Writing were all significantly under-weighted by IV alone.

Language subjects

Language subjects are handled differently depending on the subject:

Spanish uses the blended IC+IV weight path — its scaled demand gives it a 3.8% share of total demand (coincidentally unchanged from IV alone). No language interest rate is applied because real observed demand exists. Spanish is available in the US and Europe only (excluded from Asia). Note: the Europe Spanish estimate is a modest overstatement since most EU bases are in Germany, Italy and the UK, not Spain — though two US bases do exist in Spain (Naval Station Rota and Morón Air Base).

German, Italian, Japanese and Korean have no meaningful baseline demand data. Their demand is calculated using the language interest rate (default 25%) — the estimated fraction of eligible-country students who want host-country language tutoring — applied on top of the country-weight filter:

LanguageRegionCountry weightBasis
GermanEurope40% of EU studentsGermany ~35,000 US troops (largest EU base)

The language interest rate control does not affect Spanish or any non-language subject.

SAT & ACT — international adjustment

Overseas demand for SAT and ACT is set to 60% of the US weight per student. DoDEA (Department of Defense Education Activity) explicitly covers SAT/ACT fees for overseas military families and offers exam administration on base.

⚠ SAT and ACT data gap: Neither subject was observed in the 221,727-session dataset (39 subjects were present). They may route through a different channel than live chat. Demand estimates are retained in the model but treat headcount for these subjects with caution.
3
How Tutor Capacity Is Calculated
⌄

US — remaining hours method

The US uses actual data from the tutor utilization import rather than a theoretical capacity estimate. The key value is remaining hours per week per subject — the difference between tutors' desired hours and their currently utilized hours. This is real spare time in the existing pool, already net of all existing 2025 student demand.

US spare capacity formula Spare sessions/wk = Remaining hours/wk × Utilization ceiling × % willing × Concurrent multiplier ÷ Subjects per tutor

÷ (session duration in hours) to convert hours → sessions
1
Remaining hours/wk — from Tutor Utilization import (week of 3/22/26). Already net of existing demand.
2
Utilization ceiling (default 75%) — only this fraction of remaining hours is offered to DOW. The other 25% is held back as a buffer for demand spikes, tutor no-shows, and organic platform growth. Industry norm for on-demand platforms: 70–80%. Running at 100% causes stockouts when demand surges.
3
% willing (default 80%) — US tutors choose whether to accept DOW work. UK/PH tutors are 100% available since they are contracted exclusively for DOW.
4
Concurrent multiplier (default 1.2×) — live chat tutors can handle multiple sessions simultaneously. Validated from data: 15.6% of tutors ran concurrent sessions, 26.6% of sessions overlapped. 1.2× reflects the average effective throughput uplift across the pool.
5
Subjects per tutor (default 4) — divides the total spare hours by the average number of subjects each tutor covers, to avoid double-counting the same tutor across multiple subject rows. Validated: actual average 3.43 subjects/tutor from session history.

Europe (UK) and Asia (Philippines) — sessions per tutor per week

These regions have zero existing tutors, so there is no utilization data to draw from. Capacity is set directly via the EU/AS sessions per tutor per week dropdown.

EU/AS capacity formula Effective capacity per tutor/wk = Sessions per tutor/wk × Concurrent multiplier
Tutors needed per subject = Weekly demand ÷ Effective capacity per tutor/wk

The utilization ceiling does not apply to EU/AS — these tutors are contracted specifically and exclusively for DOW, so their capacity is sized directly to demand rather than carved out of existing availability.

Key difference: US uses real remaining hours (bottom-up, from actual data). EU/AS uses a top-down sessions-per-tutor assumption because no utilization data exists yet. The utilization ceiling is the most impactful supply-side lever for US headcount — changing it from 75% to 100% can swing US contracts from hundreds to zero.
4
Headcount & Contracting Math
⌄
Important: The last column in the US table shows tutor-subject combos, not unique people. One tutor covering 4 subjects = 4 combos. The summary cards show combos needed, contracts to place, and the Nov 1 subset. Contracts is the final unique-person number after deduplication and attrition gross-up: combos ÷ subjects per tutor = unique tutors ÷ (1 − attrition) = contracts.
1
Tutor-subject combos needed per subject = Gap sessions/wk ÷ effective sessions per tutor/wk. Minimum floor of 1 tutor per subject (configurable). These are combos, not people.
2
Unique active tutors = Total combos ÷ avg subjects per tutor. This deduplicates — a tutor covering 4 subjects fills 4 combos but is only 1 person.
3
Contracts to place = Unique active tutors ÷ (1 − attrition rate). Grosses up for the expected dropout rate between contracting and Nov 1 launch. Default 20%.
Summary reconciliation (example at base settings) Total tutor-subject combos ÷ 4 subjects/tutor = Unique active tutors
Unique active tutors ÷ (1 − 20%) = Contracts to place

Approval rate (the funnel from applicant to contracted tutor) is not included — that is the supply team's operational responsibility. The model outputs the number of successful contracts needed, not the number of applications to generate.

Cost Calculation
⌄
Cost formula Annual cost = Annual sessions × Session duration (hours) × Tutor rate ($/hr)
RegionRateBasis
UK (Europe)$18/hrCompetitive UK market rate; above minimum wage with quality targeting
Philippines (Asia)$10/hrTop-tier Philippines rate; competitive for quality English-proficient tutors
USNo incremental costExisting tutors on existing pay — DOW is additive work, not a new expense

Cost scales with demand (penetration rate × sessions per user × student population), not headcount. Tutors are paid per session, not per hour of availability. The Cost tab in the model shows a weighted average session duration across all subjects in each region, since HS subjects run longer than ES/MS subjects.

Contracting Timeline
⌄

Hiring window

RegionWindowRationale
UK (Europe)July – September 2026Aligned with US window. ICA work and legal review can run in parallel during June.
Philippines (Asia)July – September 2026Same as UK. All three regions hiring in parallel simplifies pipeline management.
USJuly – September 2026 (standard)Standard window. Run rate stress test in the Timeline tab will flag if a high-demand scenario requires earlier start.

Day-one adoption & two-stage contracting

Not all contracts need to be placed before Nov 1. The model separates the full peak-sized target from the Nov 1 launch target using the day-one adoption level (default 35%). This reflects the reality that new programs rarely hit full utilization on day one.

Example at base settings (35% day-one adoption): If the model projects 200 full contracts for a region, only 70 need to be active by Nov 1. The remaining 130 can be contracted in January 2027 as adoption grows — no need to over-hire before launch.

This also means the contracting timeline depends on which target you use. The Timeline tab shows both the Nov 1 combo target and the full peak target, with separate bars for each stage.

US run rate stress test

US timing is validated against real monthly contracting run rates from March 2026 data. The Timeline tab calculates months needed per subject (combos needed by Nov 1 ÷ run rate) and flags in red any subject that requires more months than remain in the Jul–Sep window. Recommend refreshing run rates in June 2026 before hiring starts — March actuals will be stale by then.

SAT (10 combos/mo) and ACT (5 combos/mo) have the lowest run rates. At high demand scenarios these subjects drive the earliest required start date.

Why the Nov 1 launch date is not a soft start

From 2 years of session history, November runs at 1.31× the annual average. November and December together represent 20.3% of annual demand. Tutors must be active and tested by launch day — not still onboarding.

UK/PH monthly pace is assumed linear across the Jul–Sep window. No historical international run rate exists. Pace assumptions should be validated once pipeline data is available.

Dropdown Controls Reference
⌄

All controls update every output in real time. Controls are grouped by region — US-only, EU/AS-only, and all-regions. Changes to demand inputs affect headcount and cost. Changes to supply inputs primarily affect headcount.

🇺🇸 United States — Demand

US Demand
Student penetration rate
Default: 10%
% of 888K US DOW students who ever use live chat. Military families are a distinct demographic — track actual sign-up in the first 30 days and update.
US Demand
Sessions per active user per year
Default: 9 sessions/yr
How often students who use the service come back. Separated from penetration so both assumptions can be stressed independently. Power users and light users behave very differently.

🇺🇸 United States — Supply

US Supply
Tutors willing to take live chat
Default: 80%
Existing US tutors who opt in to DOW work. UK/PH tutors are always 100% (contracted exclusively for DOW). Based on assumption — a tutor pulse survey would validate this.
US Supply
Max utilization ceiling
Default: 75% — recommended
Caps the fraction of US spare hours offered to DOW. The remaining 25% is a buffer for demand spikes and tutor dropoff. Industry norm: 70–80%. This is the most impactful US lever — moving from 75% to 100% can eliminate US contracts entirely at base demand.

🌍🌏 Europe & Asia — Demand

EU/AS Demand
Student penetration rate
Default: 10%
% of eligible EU/AS students who use live chat. Can be set lower than US to reflect slower adoption in a new market with no existing VT brand recognition.
EU/AS Demand
Sessions per active user per year
Default: 9 sessions/yr
Session frequency for international active users. Same options as US — both can be dialed independently.
EU/AS Demand
Language interest rate
Default: 25%
Applies only to German, Italian, Japanese and Korean — not Spanish (which uses real IV data). Fraction of eligible-country students who want host-country language tutoring. Does not affect any non-language subject.

🌍🌏 Europe & Asia — Supply

EU/AS Supply
Sessions per tutor per week
Default: 7/wk (P75 from actual data)
How many sessions each UK or PH tutor handles per week. Validated from US data: median 2, mean 7.6, P75 = 7. New international tutors may start closer to median for the first 60–90 days.
EU/AS Supply
Avg concurrent chats per tutor
Default: 1.2× (validated)
Multiplier on effective tutor throughput. 1.2× reflects ~26% session overlap observed in actual data. Applied to both US and EU/AS capacity calculations.
