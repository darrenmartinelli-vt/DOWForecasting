Program Overview
⌄

The DOW (Department of Defense) program extends Varsity Tutors live chat tutoring to military-connected families across three regions. This model forecasts how many tutors need to be contracted — by region — to handle projected demand at the November 1, 2026 launch date.

RegionStudent populationShareTutor sourceSubject scope
United States888,00095.2%Existing US poolAll 29 subjects
Europe (UK)25,0002.7%New UK hiresAll 29 excl. Japanese & Korean
Asia (Philippines)20,0002.1%New PH hiresAll 29 excl. Spanish, German & Italian

The 29 subjects span six groups: Elementary (5), Middle School (5), HS Math (5), HS Humanities (2), HS Science (4), Test Prep (2), Special Ed (1), and Languages (5 — with region-specific availability).

Spanish is available in the US and Europe only — excluded from Asia as it is not relevant for military families based in Japan or South Korea.
2
How Demand Is Calculated
⌄

For every subject and every region, the model calculates how many live chat sessions per week will be needed at launch.

Core demand formula Weekly sessions = Student population × Usage rate × Annual sessions/student × Subject weight ÷ 52

Subject demand weights

Each subject's share of total demand is derived from its share of instant video (IV) requests across the platform — 38,000 IV requests across all 29 subjects. This is the best available proxy for live chat demand mix, since live chat and instant video serve similar on-demand needs.

Language subjects

Language subjects apply an additional country-weight filter, since not all students in a region will want the same language. Population splits are based on US troop presence:

LanguageRegionCountry weightBasis
GermanEurope40% of EU studentsGermany ~35,000 US troops (largest EU base)
ItalianEurope30% of EU studentsItaly ~12,000 US troops
JapaneseAsia60% of Asia studentsJapan ~54,000 US troops (largest Asia base)
KoreanAsia40% of Asia studentsSouth Korea ~28,000 US troops
SpanishUS + Europe100% of eligible studentsWidely relevant; excluded from Asia

Language demand is then further filtered by the language interest rate (default 25%) — the estimated fraction of eligible-country students who actually want language tutoring.

SAT & ACT — international adjustment

Overseas demand for SAT and ACT is set to 60% of the US weight per student. DoDEA (Department of Defense Education Activity) explicitly covers SAT/ACT fees for overseas military families and offers exam administration on base.

⚠ SAT and ACT data gap: Neither subject was observed in the 230,602-session dataset (39 subjects were present). They may route through a different channel than live chat. Demand estimates are retained in the model but treat headcount for these subjects with caution.
3
How Tutor Capacity Is Calculated
⌄

US — remaining hours method

The US uses actual data from the tutor utilization import rather than a theoretical capacity estimate. The key value is remaining hours per week per subject — the difference between tutors' desired hours and their currently utilized hours. This is real spare time in the existing pool, already net of all existing 2025 student demand.

US spare capacity formula Spare sessions/wk = Remaining hours/wk × Utilization ceiling × % willing × Concurrent multiplier ÷ Subjects per tutor

÷ (session duration in hours) to convert hours → sessions
1
Remaining hours/wk — from Tutor Utilization import (week of 3/22/26). Already net of existing demand.
2
Utilization ceiling (default 75%) — only this fraction of remaining hours is offered to DOW. The other 25% is held back as a buffer for demand spikes, tutor no-shows, and organic platform growth. Industry norm for on-demand platforms: 70–80%. Running at 100% causes stockouts when demand surges.
3
% willing (default 60%) — US tutors choose whether to accept DOW work. UK/PH tutors are 100% available since they are contracted exclusively for DOW.
4
Concurrent multiplier (default 1.2×) — live chat tutors can handle multiple sessions simultaneously. Validated from data: 15.6% of tutors ran concurrent sessions, 26.6% of sessions overlapped. 1.2× reflects the average effective throughput uplift across the pool.
5
Subjects per tutor (default 4) — divides the total spare hours by the average number of subjects each tutor covers, to avoid double-counting the same tutor across multiple subject rows. Validated: actual average 3.43 subjects/tutor from session history.

Europe (UK) and Asia (Philippines) — sessions per tutor per week

These regions have zero existing tutors, so there is no utilization data to draw from. Capacity is set directly via the EU/AS sessions per tutor per week dropdown.

EU/AS capacity formula Effective capacity per tutor/wk = Sessions per tutor/wk × Concurrent multiplier
Tutors needed per subject = Weekly demand ÷ Effective capacity per tutor/wk

The utilization ceiling does not apply to EU/AS — these tutors are contracted specifically and exclusively for DOW, so their capacity is sized directly to demand rather than carved out of existing availability.

Key difference: US uses real remaining hours (bottom-up, from actual data). EU/AS uses a top-down sessions-per-tutor assumption because no utilization data exists yet. The utilization ceiling is the most impactful supply-side lever for US headcount — changing it from 75% to 100% can swing US contracts from hundreds to zero.
4
Headcount & Contracting Math
⌄
Important: The "Subject-slots needed" column in the US table shows tutor-subject assignments, not unique people. One tutor covering 4 subjects counts as 4 slots. The summary contract count in the header card is the final unique-person number after deduplication and attrition gross-up.
1
Subject-slots needed per subject = Gap sessions/wk ÷ effective sessions per tutor/wk. Minimum floor of 1 tutor per subject (configurable). These are slots, not people.
2
Unique active tutors = Total subject-slots ÷ avg subjects per tutor. This deduplicates — a tutor covering 4 subjects fills 4 slots but is only 1 person.
3
Contracts to place = Unique active tutors ÷ (1 − attrition rate). Grosses up for the expected dropout rate between contracting and Nov 1 launch. Default 20%.
Summary reconciliation (example at base settings) Total subject-slots ÷ 4 subjects/tutor = Unique active tutors
Unique active tutors ÷ (1 − 20%) = Contracts to place

Approval rate (the funnel from applicant to contracted tutor) is not included — that is the supply team's operational responsibility. The model outputs the number of successful contracts needed, not the number of applications to generate.

Cost Calculation
⌄
Cost formula Annual cost = Annual sessions × Session duration (hours) × Tutor rate ($/hr)
RegionRateBasis
UK (Europe)$18/hrCompetitive UK market rate; above minimum wage with quality targeting
Philippines (Asia)$10/hrTop-tier Philippines rate; competitive for quality English-proficient tutors
USNo incremental costExisting tutors on existing pay — DOW is additive work, not a new expense

Cost scales with demand (usage rate and student population), not headcount. Tutors are paid per session, not per hour of availability. The Cost tab in the model shows a weighted average session duration across all subjects in each region, since HS subjects run longer than ES/MS subjects.

Contracting Timeline
⌄
RegionWindowRationale
UK (Europe)April – October 2026No existing pipeline. 7 months needed to build sourcing, screening, and onboarding from scratch. Feb–Mar used for setup.
Philippines (Asia)April – October 2026Same as UK — new country, new pipeline. Requires ICA work, payment systems, and legal review.
USJuly – September 2026Existing infrastructure. 3-month window is sufficient for smaller volumes typically needed.

US timing is grounded in real monthly run rates per subject from March 2026 contracting data. SAT (10/mo) and ACT (5/mo) have the lowest run rates — if US contracts are required for these subjects, hiring must start earliest.

UK/PH monthly pace is set by dividing total contracts evenly across the 7-month window since no historical international run rate data exists.

9
Dropdown Controls Reference
⌄

All controls update every output in real time. Changes to demand inputs affect headcount and cost equally. Changes to supply inputs primarily affect headcount; cost is only affected by concurrent chats (via headcount) and session duration (directly).

Demand
US usage rate
Default: 6.5% · 13 sessions/yr
Fraction of 888K US DOW students who use live chat, and how many times per year. Scales total US session demand proportionally.
Demand
Europe & Asia usage rate
Default: 6.5% · 13 sessions/yr
Same as US but applied to international populations. Can be set lower to reflect slower initial adoption in a new market.
Demand
Language interest rate
Default: 25%
Fraction of eligible-country students who want language tutoring. Applied on top of usage rate and country-weight. 25% is conservative.
Supply
US tutors willing to take live chat
Default: 60%
Existing US tutors who opt in to DOW work. UK/PH tutors are always 100% (contracted exclusively for DOW). Increase if active outreach is planned.
Supply
Max utilization ceiling (US)
Default: 75% — recommended
Caps the fraction of US spare hours offered to DOW. The remaining % is a buffer for demand spikes and tutor dropoff. Industry norm: 70–80%. This is the most impactful US lever — moving from 75% to 100% can eliminate US contracts entirely at base demand.
Supply
EU/AS sessions per tutor per week
Default: 7/wk (P75 from actual data)
How many sessions each UK or PH tutor handles per week. Validated from US data: median is 2, mean is 7.6, P75 is 7. Setting this lower is more conservative; higher assumes more active tutors.
Supply
Avg concurrent chats per tutor
Default: 1.2× (validated)
Multiplier on effective tutor throughput. 1.2× reflects the ~26% session overlap rate observed in actual data. Applied to both US spare capacity and EU/AS capacity.
Supply
Avg DOW subjects per tutor
Default: 4 (validated avg: 3.43)
Each tutor covers this many subjects. Deduplicates the subject-slot count into unique people. Validated from session history — actual average is 3.43 subjects/tutor.
Supply
Pre-launch attrition
Default: 20%
Fraction of contracted tutors who drop out before Nov 1 launch. Contracts are grossed up to ensure the active pool target is still met despite attrition.
Supply
Min tutors per subject
Default: 1
Floor on tutors per subject. Prevents any subject from showing zero even if modeled demand is very small. Useful for ensuring baseline coverage.
